Weighted blankets are therapeutic blankets designed to provide gentle, even pressure across the body, creating a sensation known as deep touch pressure (DTP). Typically filled with materials like glass beads or plastic pellets, these blankets are engineered to weigh between 5 to 30 pounds, with the weight evenly distributed in stitched pockets or compartments. The concept behind weighted blankets is rooted in the theory of sensory integration, which suggests that deep pressure stimulation can help calm the nervous system and improve overall well-being. This sensation of being held or hugged by the blanket is believed to release neurotransmitters like serotonin and dopamine, promoting relaxation and reducing stress and anxiety levels. Weighted blankets have gained popularity as a natural, non-pharmacological way to alleviate symptoms of anxiety, insomnia, and certain sensory processing disorders, making them a popular choice for both adults and children seeking improved sleep quality and emotional comfort.

These blankets come in various sizes and materials, allowing users to choose the one that best suits their preferences and needs. While weighted blankets have shown promise in improving sleep and reducing anxiety for some individuals, it's essential to select an appropriate weight that is around 10% of your body weight to ensure effectiveness and safety. Although they can be beneficial, weighted blankets may not be suitable for everyone, especially those with respiratory or mobility issues, so consulting with a healthcare professional before use is advisable. Overall, weighted blankets offer a potentially soothing and comforting solution for those looking to manage stress, sleep disturbances, or sensory sensitivities naturally, without relying on medication or other interventions.
